# Manufacturer Case Study

## Client

Industrial manufacturer producing custom products with multiple production, quality, and shipping steps.

## The Problem

The client had limited visibility into production, quality, inventory, and machine performance. Operators manually selected machine settings based on experience, finished goods inventory was difficult to track in real time, and management lacked reliable data on downtime, scrap, and production capacity.

- Machine parameters were selected through trial and error
- Limited traceability between production, QA, and shipping
- Real-time inventory was difficult to measure
- Downtime and production capacity were not well understood
- High scrap rates increased material costs

## Solution

Designed and implemented a shop-floor data platform that connected production, quality, inventory, and shipping data into a single workflow. Key capabilities included:

### Data Collection App

Built a digitized data collection app so operators could capture machine parameters and product measurements for every production run, creating a historical dataset linking machine settings to production outcomes.

### End-to-End Traceability

Assigned a unique QR code to each product at production and scanned it throughout manufacturing, quality control, inventory, and shipping, delivering complete traceability across the process.

### Automatic Label Printing & QR Inventory

Automated QR label printing on the shop floor and tied each scan to inventory, so stock counts and product location updated automatically as items moved through the plant.

### Real-Time Inventory Tracking

Automatically updated inventory quantities as products moved through production, QA, storage, and shipment, giving accurate visibility into work-in-progress and finished goods.

### Operational Analytics Dashboard

Consolidated production, quality, downtime, and inventory data into reporting dashboards used by supervisors and plant leadership.

## Results

### Reduced Material Usage

Analysis of historical production data identified machine settings that consistently produced quality products while minimizing raw material consumption. Combined with operator training, material usage decreased by approximately 1.3%.

### Reduced Scrap

Production data revealed that certain machine configurations generated higher defect rates. Standardizing optimal operating parameters reduced scrap by approximately 20%.

### Improved Throughput

Analysis showed that operating machines slightly slower produced fewer defects and less rework, resulting in more total sellable products over time. 

### Better Capacity Planning

Downtime and production data provided a clearer understanding of actual manufacturing capacity, enabling more accurate production scheduling and delivery commitments.
